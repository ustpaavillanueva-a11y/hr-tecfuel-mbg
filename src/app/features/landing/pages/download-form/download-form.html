Statutory Benefits Deadline

Submission Date: Every 15th of the Month.

Payment Window: Please settle payments on or before the 14th to ensure funds are cleared and posted by the official deadline.

Coverage: Includes SSS, PhilHealth, and HDMF (Pag-IBIG) contributions.

Holiday Pay Policy

1. Regular Holidays

These are fixed dates of national significance such as New Year's Day and Independence Day.

If it falls on a Rest Day: If worked, the rate is 260% (Daily Rate X 200% X 130).

If Unworked: The employee receives 100% of their daily wage, provided they were present or on paid leave on the workday immediately preceding the holiday.

If Worked: The employee receives 200% of their daily wage for the first 8 hours.

Overtime (OT): Any work beyond 8 hours earns an additional 30% of the hourly rate on that day (Hourly Rate X 200% X 130).

2. Special Non-Working Days

These are often declared via Presidential Proclamation such as Ninoy Aquino Day and All Saints' Day.

If it falls on a Rest Day: If worked, the rate is 150% of the daily wage.

If Unworked: The No Work, No Pay principle applies unless company policy or a CBA states otherwise.

If Worked: The employee receives an additional 30% of their daily wage, totaling 130%.

Overtime (OT): Any work beyond 8 hours earns an additional 30% of the hourly rate on that day (Hourly Rate X 130% X 130).

3. Special Working Holidays

On these days, no premium pay is required.

Work Rule: If an employee works, they receive only their regular daily wage (100%).

If Unworked: No pay under standard workday rules.

4. Straight Duty

First 8 Hours: Paid at the regular daily rate.

Next Shift on the Same Day: These are treated as Overtime (OT).

Regular Day: Hourly Rate X 125%

Rest Day/Holiday: Hourly Rate X 130% on top of the holiday premium.

Night Differential: If the straight duty extends into the 10:00 PM to 6:00 AM window, the 10% Night Shift Differential must be added to the OT rate.

Leave Entitlement and Accrual Policy

All eligible employees are entitled to a quarterly leave allocation consisting of two (2) Vacation Leave (VL) days and two (2) Sick Leave (SL) days.
